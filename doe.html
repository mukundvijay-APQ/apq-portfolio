DOE: Optimizing Ultrasonic Welding for a Waterproof Enclosure

Mukund Vijay · Fictional scenario

Situation

A next-generation fitness tracker required IP68 waterproof rating. Initial PVT builds showed 8% water ingress failure rate — far above the 0.5% target. OFAT experiments had failed to improve yield after 3 weeks of iteration.

DOE Design & Results

Factor Selection

FACTORLOW (−1)HIGH (+1)
A: Weld amplitude30 μm45 μm
B: Weld force200 N350 N
C: Hold time0.3 sec0.8 sec
D: Energy limit150 J250 J

Experiment Structure

Full factorial 2⁴ = 16 runs × 3 replicates = 48 total units. Response: seal leak rate (cc/min at 30 psi). Pass criteria: ≤0.05 cc/min. Randomized run order.

KEY FINDING: A×B INTERACTION

High amplitude + high force produced seal quality significantly better than either factor alone would predict. This interaction explained why OFAT failed — adjusting amplitude or force independently missed the synergistic optimum.

Hold time (Factor C) was not statistically significant (p=0.42), eliminating an unnecessary variable from the control plan.

Optimal Settings

Amplitude: 42 μm (±2 μm process window)

Force: 320 N (±20 N process window)

Hold time: 0.5 sec (midpoint — not significant)

Energy limit: 230 J (±15 J process window)

Confirmation Results

METRICBEFORE DOEAFTER DOE
Water ingress failure rate8.0%0.3%
Mean leak rate0.072 cc/min0.012 cc/min
Cpk (leak rate)0.622.14
Cycle time impactBaseline+0.2 sec

Key Takeaways

The full factorial DOE resolved in 2 days what 3 weeks of OFAT had failed to solve. The interaction effect was invisible to one-variable-at-a-time testing. Total DOE cost: ~$3K (48 units + 2 days engineering time). Value: transformed a build-stopping 8% failure rate into production-ready 0.3%.
