LEAN: Eliminating 40% of NPI-to-Sustaining Handoff Waste

Mukund Vijay · Fictional scenario

Situation

A consumer electronics company launching 3–4 products annually experienced a recurring pattern: quality issues resolved during NPI resurfaced 3–6 months after production handoff. The sustaining quality team spent an estimated 40% of their first 6 months re-discovering and re-solving problems NPI had already addressed.

LEAN Analysis & Countermeasures

VALUE STREAM SUMMARY

Total value-add time: 20 hours

Total waste time: 162+ hours (12 days wait + 150 hours rework/search/duplication)

Process efficiency: 11% — 89% waste

Root Causes (5 Whys)

Why do sustaining teams re-investigate? → NPI documentation scattered across email, Confluence, shared drives

Why is it scattered? → No standardized handoff template or required deliverable checklist

Why no template? → Each program manager creates their own documentation approach

Why? → The organization never defined what "complete handoff" means

Why not? → Historically verbal/tribal knowledge transfer; organization grew faster than its processes

Countermeasure 1: Quality Passport

Mandatory handoff document containing:

  • Known issue register with status, root cause, and verification evidence
  • FMEA (current revision with all RPN updates from NPI)
  • Control plan with inspection points, frequencies, and reaction plans
  • Supplier quality summary: approved vendor list, open SCARs
  • CTQ parameter list with target values and current Cpk
  • Test coverage matrix: which tests catch which failure modes

Countermeasure 2: Overlap Period

Replaced "hard cutover" with 4-week overlap:

  • Weeks 1–2: Sustaining engineer shadows NPI engineer
  • Weeks 3–4: NPI engineer shadows sustaining engineer
  • Daily 15-minute standup to transfer tacit knowledge

Countermeasure 3: Completeness Gate

No production transfer approved until sustaining quality manager signs off that the Quality Passport is complete and the overlap period is finished.

Results (Next 2 Product Launches)

METRICBEFORE LEANAFTER LEAN
Handoff waste time162+ hours~40 hours
Process efficiency11%33%
Known issues rediscovered60–70%<10%
Time to full productivity6 months6 weeks
Cost savings per launch—~$180K

Key Takeaways

The biggest waste wasn't a factory-floor problem — it was an information-flow problem. LEAN's value stream mapping exposed that 89% of the handoff process was waste. The overlap period was the highest-impact change: tacit knowledge can't be captured in documents. Side-by-side gemba time was the only way to transfer it.
