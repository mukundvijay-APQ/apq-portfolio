8D: Charging Dock Overheating in a Smart Ring

Mukund Vijay · Fictional scenario

Situation

A smart ring company received a surge of customer reports about the wireless charging dock becoming "too hot to touch" during charging. Social media amplified the issue with 200+ Reddit posts in one week. The quality team needed to move fast — this was a potential safety issue with brand reputation at stake.

8D Investigation

D1: Establish the Team

Cross-functional 8D team assembled within 4 hours: Quality Engineering (lead), Electrical Engineering, Firmware, Supplier Quality, CM liaison (Estonia), and Customer Experience. Executive sponsor: VP of Hardware.

D2: PROBLEM STATEMENT

What: Charging dock surface temperature exceeding 55°C during normal charging cycle (spec limit: 45°C)

Where: Units manufactured at Estonia CM, weeks 42–48, across all ring sizes

When: Reports began ~6 weeks after holiday launch shipments

Magnitude: ~0.4% of units sold; 340 returns, 2,100 complaints

D3: Interim Containment

  • Pushed firmware update limiting charge current to 80% of nominal
  • Customer communication via app: "Charging optimization update"
  • Held 4,200 docks at CM warehouse pending investigation
  • Expedited 50 affected units to failure analysis lab

D4: Root Cause Analysis

Lab analysis of 50 returned docks using thermal imaging, X-ray inspection, and electrical characterization:

  • Thermal imaging identified hotspot at charging coil — 12°C above nominal
  • X-ray revealed inconsistent ferrite shielding placement — 0.3–0.8mm offset
  • Offset caused eddy current heating in adjacent copper ground plane
  • Traced to CM assembly fixture wear — alignment pins worn 0.4mm over 80,000 cycles

ROOT CAUSE

Direct: Ferrite shield misalignment causing parasitic eddy current heating in charging dock ground plane

Contributing: Assembly fixture pin wear exceeding tolerance; no fixture maintenance schedule in CM's preventive maintenance program for this station

D5: Permanent Corrective Action

  • Replaced worn fixture pins; implemented maintenance schedule (every 50,000 cycles)
  • Added post-assembly thermal scan at CM — 100% inspection initially
  • Tightened ferrite placement tolerance from ±1.0mm to ±0.5mm
  • Added firmware thermal monitoring: auto-throttle at 42°C

D6: Validate

D7: Prevent Recurrence

  • Updated FMEA: added "fixture wear causing component misalignment" as failure mode
  • Established fixture preventive maintenance program across all CM stations
  • Created "thermal CTQ checklist" for all future inductive charging products

D8: Recognize the Team

8D cycle completed in 14 days. Team recognized in company all-hands. The firmware thermal throttling feature was promoted as a customer safety improvement.

Key Takeaways

The interim containment (firmware throttle) bought time for proper root cause analysis without requiring a full product hold. The fixture maintenance gap was a systemic issue — the 8D surfaced a broader preventive maintenance gap that would have eventually caused quality issues on other product lines.
