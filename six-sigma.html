SIX SIGMA — DMAIC

Six Sigma: BLE Antenna Impedance from 4σ to 6σ

Mukund Vijay · Fictional scenario

Situation

A smart ring's BLE antenna impedance needed to match 50Ω for optimal wireless performance. Field data showed 2.3% of units experienced intermittent Bluetooth disconnections. Process capability was 4.1σ (Cpk = 1.37). Target: achieve 6σ capability (Cpk ≥ 2.0).

DMAIC Cycle

DEFINE

Y (output): Antenna impedance at 2.4 GHz — target 50Ω, spec 45–55Ω

Baseline: Mean = 50.8Ω, σ = 1.22Ω, Cpk = 1.37 (4.1σ)

Business case: 2.3% × $85 warranty × 2M units/yr = $3.9M annual quality cost

Measure

Gage R&R: 8.2% of total variation — acceptable (<10%). Linearity: 0.3Ω bias corrected via calibration offset. Collected 500 measurements across 3 shifts, 2 lines, 5 days.

Analyze — Multi-Vari

SOURCE% OF VARIATIONROOT CAUSE
PCB trace width42%Etching process variation
Encapsulation thickness31%Epoxy dispensing volume
Component placement18%Pick-and-place accuracy for 0201 caps
Measurement / other9%Gage R&R + environmental

Improve

  • PCB trace: Upgraded from spray etch to puddle etch (Cpk 1.1→1.8); added impedance coupon to every panel
  • Encapsulation: Switched to closed-loop weight verification + laser profilometry (100% measurement)
  • Component placement: Tightened from ±75μm to ±40μm with AOI verification

Control & Results

METRICBEFORE (4.1σ)AFTER (6.2σ)
Mean impedance50.8Ω50.1Ω
Standard deviation1.22Ω0.78Ω
Cpk1.372.07
BLE disconnect failures2.3%0.08%
Annual warranty savings—$3.76M on $120K investment

Key Takeaways

The multi-vari analysis was the turning point — without it, the team would have focused on component placement (18%) rather than the true dominant source: PCB trace width (42%). Achieving 6σ didn't require exotic technology — it required disciplined measurement, focused variation reduction on the vital few sources, and a control plan to sustain gains.
